<threat_model>
## Trust Boundaries

| Boundary | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Reporter → Coordinator | Three reporters POST signals over HTTP to coordinator on localhost. No auth (SPEC). |
| ETM → Coordinator | ETM session_id = `CLAUDE_SESSION_ID || SESSION_ID` env var. A malicious local process could spawn an ETM with a forged env var, signalling a fake healthy session. |

## STRIDE Threat Register

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-33-04-01 | S (Spoofing) | A malicious local process could pose as a reporter and POST signals | accept | Localhost-only deployment per SPEC. Documented residual risk (also covered by T-33-02-01). |
| T-33-04-02 | T (Tampering) | A reporter could send `status: 'healthy'` for a session it doesn't own | accept | Same scope as Spoofing. SPEC R6 grep gate ensures consumers don't fall back to 'healthy' on coordinator-side checks; reporter-side false positives are bounded by the 15s staleness threshold (D-10) — a session reported `running` will flip to `stopped` if heartbeats stop. |
| T-33-04-03 | I (Information Disclosure) | Reporter logs may include transcriptPath / projectPath | accept | Logs go to local `.logs/`, same as today. No new exposure surface. |
| T-33-04-04 | D (DoS) | A reporter loop could flood the coordinator with signals | mitigate | Coordinator is single-process, in-memory state. ETM heartbeats are 4 s; statusline-monitor heartbeats are 2 s. Coordinator's `express.json({ limit: '1mb' })` caps per-request body. CONTEXT.md "~12 POSTs/min/reporter — trivial overhead". |
| T-33-04-05 | R (Repudiation) | A reporter could hide a failure by simply not POSTing | mitigate | 15 s staleness threshold (D-10) flips silent reporters to `stopped`. SPEC R6 ensures `unknown` propagates rather than 'healthy'. |
</threat_model>
