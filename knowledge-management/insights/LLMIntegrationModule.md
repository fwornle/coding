### Design Decisions and Trade‑offs  

* **Caching vs. Freshness** – Caching reduces cost and latency but may serve stale LLM responses for rapidly evolving data. The trade‑off is mitigated by cache invalidation policies (not described).  
* **Message Queue Decoupling** – Improves resilience and scalability but adds operational complexity (queue management, ordering guarantees).  
* **File‑Based Error Logging + Notification** – Provides durable records and immediate alerts, at the cost of duplicated error handling pathways.  
* **Graph Database Choice** – Enables complex relationship queries but introduces a separate data store that must be maintained and backed up.
